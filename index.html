Argument for Watching Movies

The use of films provides sensing through the arts as well as listening. Through music, cinematography, and acting, films make the audience experience the emotions and themes portrayed, thereby creating a multi-sensory experience that arrests the attention of the audience. It leads to the audience, making them a more active part of the story than just telling. Also, the films can be a link between the different cultures and languages, thus, they can be understandable by other people that are not familiar with them. The visual medium gives the possibility to describe action sequences or feelings whose depiction in text could be quite problematic. For the audience that cannot read well or have little time to read, movies are a straightforward yet intriguing way of exploring stories. While books delve into the inner aspects of characters, movies provide a social experience - group watching of films lead to connections and discussions. A movie that is watched together creates the shared experience, which later the friends and family talk about it with joy and understanding.
